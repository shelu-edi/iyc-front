Enhancing Entrepreneurial Competence

Knowledge, skills, attitudes, values, and behaviours that people require to successfully do a specific task or activity is a reference to competence. The exposure to an entrepreneurship program, in this case one that focuses on both the classroom and intense experience learning, can improve one's entrepreneurial competencies.

The younger generation appears to improve in exhibiting certain competencies when placed in a framework that focuses on experiencing and carrying out practical tasks that support entrepreneurial objectives. The International Youth Council proposes to develop suitable framework for the future youth generations based on the PESTEL analysis. Enhancing Entrepreneurial Competence is an important focus at the IYC, as contributes towards achieving and implementing the Sustainable Development Goals.

Education For Better Future

People of various ages and socioeconomic backgrounds need education to succeed in life. However, there is growing agreement that our current educational systems fall short and that we need to repurpose education and rethink the organization of learning to meet the challenges of the 21st century. This is because demographic, environmental, economic, socio-political, and technological changes are occurring at an accelerating rate, along with the risks and opportunities they bring. Accordingly, the International Youth Council collaborates with other worldwide stakeholders to uncover the flaws in the current educational systems through several studies and to provide the best systems through which the youth could improve their future possibilities.

Protecting the Environment

Our environment changes as our ways living, travelling, manufacturing and consuming goods change. This includes everything from the construction of new housing and transportation infrastructure to the construction of waste and water treatment facilities, the transition of technologies in the energy and industrial sectors, and the adoption of more intensive agricultural practices to meet societal needs. Development may result in irreversible environmental damage and serious environmental issues which will compromise health, safety and quality of living beings.

Creating Sustainable Societies

A sustainable society is defined as a world with clean air and water, people who live in harmony with the environment, consumer goods that are recycled to make new products, no use of fossil fuels, and people who live and work in a connected environment. Building this society, however, is a difficult task. In addition to clean, renewable fuels to replace traditional energy sources and secure technology for our digital connections, it will take a radical shift in perspective to prioritise this harmony. Scaling up while controlling the planet's resources to enable sustainable growth will necessitate a significant shift in perspective.

However, maintaining and preserving peace and reconciliation among humanity, while prioritising harmony through coexistence, is equally important. Accepting differences and learning to live in harmony can account for an increased pace in the development and creation of sustainable societies, especially in developed nations where diverse nationalities coexist.

The IYC aims to expose youth from rural and urban societies to advanced technologies, the digitalised world and potentially innovative technical knowledge.

Cross-cutting services

IYC offers a range of cross-cutting services to assist nations in putting their policies and programs into action. These services include facilitating networking and partnerships, producing targeted knowledge products, and conducting rigorous results monitoring and reporting at all levels. Additionally, it helps nations develop the youth policy and statistical capacities required for planning and operationalizing.

Humanitarian Support

The goal of concern is to establish a society free from oppression, fear, or poverty. It's a sizable, audacious, and ambitious vision, but one that we think is achievable with the right work. Our work can be broadly divided into two categories: development and humanitarian aid. Although those expressions may appear to be equivalent, they each serve significant purposes. Humanitarian assistance aims to prevent and improve readiness for disasters brought on by natural disasters and man-made crises by preserving life, reducing suffering, and upholding human dignity during and after these events. The fundamental humanitarian principles of humanity, impartiality, neutrality, and independence can also serve as guidelines for humanitarian aid.
