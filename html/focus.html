Our Focus
Enhancing Entrepreneurial Competence

Competence refers to the knowledge, skills, attitudes, values, and behaviors that people need to perform a specific task or activity successfully. Exposure to an entrepreneurship program, specifically one that emphasizes both classroom and intensive experience learning, may help an individual to improve his entrepreneurial competencies. These revolve around attitudes (soft skills) including perseverance, networking and self-confidence and enabling skills (hard skills) including basic start up knowledge, business planning, financial literacy and managerial skills.

When experiencing and conducting practical tasks that support entrepreneurial goals are structured, the younger generation is more advanced in exhibiting certain competencies. Based on the PESTEL analysis, the International Youth Council proposes developing an appropriate framework for future youth generations. Enhancing Entrepreneurial Competence is a key focus area at the International Youth Council because it contributes to the achievement and implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals.

Education For Better Future

People of all ages and socioeconomic backgrounds require education in order to thrive in life. However, there is growing consensus that our current educational systems fall short and that we must repurpose education and rethink the organization of learning to cater to the challenges of the 21st century. This is due to the fact that demographic, environmental, economic, sociopolitical, and technological changes are accelerating, as are the risks and opportunities they bring. As a result, the International Youth Council works with other global stakeholders to identify flaws in current educational systems through various studies and to provide the best systems through which youth can improve their future opportunities.

Protecting the Environment

The conditions of the environment change according to changes of lifestyles, traveling, manufacturing and consumption of goods. This includes everything from building new housing and transportation infrastructure to building waste and water treatment facilities, transitioning technologies in the energy and industrial sectors, and adopting more intensive agricultural practices to meet societal needs. Development may cause irreversible environmental damage as well as serious environmental issues that jeopardize the health, safety, and quality of life of living beings.

Creating Sustainable Societies

A sustainable society is referred to as one with clean air and water, people who live in harmony with nature, consumer goods that are recycled to make new products, no use of fossil fuels, and people who live and work in a connected environment. Building this society, on the other hand, is a difficult task. In addition to clean, renewable fuels to replace traditional energy sources and secure technology for our digital connections, prioritizing this harmony will require a radical shift in perspective. To enable sustainable growth, scaling up while controlling the planet's resources will necessitate a significant shift in perspective.

Establishing and preserving human peace and reconciliation, while prioritizing harmony through coexistence, is crucially significant. Accepting differences and learning to live in harmony can account for a faster rate of development and creation of sustainable societies, particularly in developed countries with diverse nationalities.

The International Youth Council targets to expose youth from rural and urban societies to advanced technologies, the digitized world, and potentially innovative technical knowledge.

Improving Physical, Mental, and Social Well-being

Health depicts a human being's wholeness, as a composite of physical, emotional, intellectual, social, and other parts, in which the interdependence of the many parts is acknowledged and accepted. Individual education has been shown to improve mental health, and its impacts are largely moderated by one's employment situation and social support structure. Education promotes physical health on an individual and community level, and its effects are moderated to some extent by economic well-being and two measures of social well-being. This method encourages the formation of relationships based on mutual respect. The three aspects of well-being are interconnected and frequently taught as a unit.

Cross-cutting services

IYC provides a variety of cross-cutting services to help nations implement their policies and programs. These services include the facilitation of networking and partnerships, the creation of targeted knowledge products, and the execution of rigorous results monitoring and reporting at all levels. Furthermore, it assists nations in developing the youth policy and statistical capacities required for planning and implementation.

Humanitarian Support

The goal of concern is to create a society free of oppression, fear, and poverty. It's a large, audacious, and ambitious vision, but one we believe is attainable with the right effort. Our work is divided into two categories: development and humanitarian aid. Although those expressions appear to be synonymous, they serve distinct functions. Humanitarian assistance aims to prevent and improve preparedness for natural disasters and man-made crises by preserving life, reducing suffering, and preserving human dignity during and after these events. Moreover, humanitarian aid can be guided by the fundamental humanitarian principles of humanity, impartiality, neutrality, and independence.
