LEADERSHIP
Achim Steiner Administrator
Achim Steiner was appointed UNDP Administrator on June 19, 2017. The United Nations General Assembly confirmed his appointment after UN Secretary-General António Guterres nominated him. In April 2021, the General Assembly confirmed his appointment to a second four-year term beginning in June 2021.

With almost three decades of expertise, Achim Steiner is a global leader in sustainable development, climate resilience, and international cooperation. He has consistently pushed to advance sustainability, economic growth, and equality for the most vulnerable people. He has been a staunch supporter of the Sustainable Development Goals.

Before joining UNDP, he served as the Director of the Oxford Martin School and a Professorial Fellow at Balliol College. Mr. Steiner has experience working for the entire UN organization, looking at global issues from both a humanitarian and a development perspective. From 2006 until 2016, he led the UNEP, which helped countries with their investments in renewable energy and green technologies. He additionally held the position of director general for the Nairobi office. Achim Steiner has held prominent positions as the Director General of the International Union for the Conservation of Nature and the Secretary General of the World Commission on Dams.

Achim Steiner has resided and worked throughout the world, including the Middle East, Africa, Latin America, and Europe. He earned his MA in School of Oriental and African Studies (SOAS) at the University of London and his MA in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics from Worcester College at Oxford University.
Usha Rao-Monari
Under-Secretary-General and Associate Administrator of UNDP
Usha Rao-Monari was appointed associate administrator of the UNDP on April 6, 2021.

More than 30 years of investment expertise, mostly in the infrastructure industry, have been accumulated by Ms. Rao-Monari, who has held a variety of notable roles. She has held several high-ranking posts in the past, including Chief Executive Officer of Global Water Development Partners, a company owned by Blackstone, and Director of the Sustainable Business Advisory Group at the International Finance Corporation, a member of the World Bank Group. Also, she worked as the Infrastructure Group Senior Advisor at Blackstone.

She formerly served on the boards of various organizations in sustainable development and held a range of advisory and board positions in water, natural capital, biodiversity, and the environment.

Ms. Rao-Monari holds master's degrees in international affairs and finance from Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs/School of Business, management studies from Mumbai, India's Jamnalal Bajaj Institute of Management, and economics with honours from Delhi University in New Delhi.

Khalida Bouzar
UN Assistant Secretary General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director of the UNDP Regional Bureau for Arab States
Khalida Bouzar has 35 years of business leadership experience, 25 of which she spent in the United Nations system. She is an expert in these fields and has dedicated her life to improving development. She is educated on building insecure governments and dealing with disagreements and their consequences.

She has worked extensively on a variety of development issues, including rural development, environment, climate change, migration, economics, and industry in Sub-Saharan Africa, Asia, Eastern Europe, and particularly the Arab area.

Prior to her appointment as Director of the UNDP Regional Bureau for Arab States, Dr. Bouzar served as Regional Director for the Near East, North Africa, and Europe at the International Fund for Agricultural Development (IFAD) from 2012. In this role, she established and managed large-scale programs and operations with a total investment volume of USD 2.6 billion across 45 countries.

Her most noteworthy achievements include establishing the Facility for Refugees, Migrants, Forced Displacement and Rural Stability (FARMS), steering the current IFAD policy for engagement in fragile states, negotiating the first-ever country program engagement with Iraq, and working in countries with little fiscal headroom like Yemen.

Prior to this, Dr. Bouzar served as the UN Environment Program's (UNEP) Deputy Director for the Division of Technology, Industry, and Economics, where he managed the development of strategy, policies, and portfolios as well as significant connections (2008–2012). Between 2004 and 2008, she led the establishment of the IFAD's Global Environment and Climate Change business operations, assisting in the mobilization of significant financing and the broader mainstreaming of these concerns throughout all of IFAD's operations.

She has also served as the International Technical Expert in Drylands Development at the Bureau for Development Policy of the UNDP, the Regional Sustainable Development Advisor at UNDP Algeria, and the Program Director for the Global Mechanism of the UNCCD.

Dr. Bouzar has a PhD in science from the University of Sciences Pierre et Marie Curie in Paris and is proficient in English, Arabic, and French.

Angelique M. Crumbly
UN Assistant Secretary-General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director of the Bureau for Management Services
Ms. Angelique M. Crumbly is the Director of the Bureau for Management Services (BMS), Assistant Administrator, and Assistant Secretary-General of the UN. As the Chief Procurement Officer of UNDP and a member of the Executive Group, Ms. Crumbly is in charge of creating and directing UNDP's overall corporate strategic directions.

The key functional areas of the bureau that Ms. Crumbly manages are financial resource management, including a budget, human resources, procurement, information technology, legal affairs, security, general operations, and multi-partner trust funds. Each of these departments has offices that report to her directly. Ms. Crumbly is also in charge of information networks, compliance and risk management for the Organizations, and the development of corporate services with a focus on the needs of the client. Ms. Crumbly has been in charge of directing the development and execution of significant organizational goals since she joined UNDP, including a new information technology strategy and people strategy. She has also volunteered to look into UNDP's legal and purchasing procedures.

Ms. Crumbly has built a career in public service and has held a variety of major positions at USAID. Before joining UNDP, she served as Senior Deputy Assistant Administrator and Performance Improvement Officer (PIO) for USAID's Bureau for Management (M). Over the years, she has held a variety of positions, such as Procurement Analyst and Contract Specialist in the Office of Acquisition and Assistance, Chief of the Policy and Technical Division in the Office of Food for Peace, Senior Advisor to the Agency Counsellor and Officer-in-Charge of the Bureau for Management, Director of the Office of Management Policy, Budget, and Performance, and Presidentially Appointed Assistant Administrator for the Bureau for Management.

As a result, she was in charge of five offices, more than 1,100 staff members, and the Agency's financial management, information technology, management services, acquisition and assistance, management policy, operational budget, and management performance.

In addition to her outstanding performance in the fields of procurement, information technology, and budget, Ms. Crumbly has led management reviews, cost-saving efforts, and streamlining activities that have contributed to the development of good business policies and management systems. She was in charge of directing the development of the Agency's operational budget and the execution of the $17 billion or so programming budget. Her leadership at USAID produced realistic commercial solutions that resulted in simplified and innovative development and humanitarian relief operations.

Her academic credentials include a Georgetown University master's in public policy management and a George Washington University bachelor's in international politics.
Ivana Zivkovic
UN Assistant Secretary General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director of the UNDP Regional Bureau for Europe and the CIS
In addition to serving as the UN Assistant Secretary-General, Ivana Zivkovic is the Regional Director of the UNDP's Regional Bureau for Europe and the Commonwealth of Independent States.

Additionally, Ms. Zivkovic serves as Co-Vice Chair of the Regional Collaborative Platform for Europe and Central Asia (RCP), a grouping of all UN agencies engaged in development activities for the 2030 Agenda. Together with the UN Economic Commission for Europe's Executive Secretary, she serves in this capacity. The chair of the RCP is the Deputy Secretary-General.

Croatian-born Mrs. Zivkovic has over 25 years of experience as a professional diplomat. Her most recent role at the Ministry of Foreign and European Affairs was Director-General for Economic Affairs and Development Cooperation. She worked in several roles in the areas of development, international trade, regional cooperation, and economic diplomacy while based in Stockholm and New York. She contributed significantly to the formation of Croatia's framework for international development cooperation. During Croatia's EU Council leadership, she was in charge of teams focusing on trade and development.

Ms. Zivkovic graduated from the Faculty of Economics at the University of Zagreb with a Bachelor of Science in Economics and a Master of Science in International Economics.
Ahunna Eziakonwa
UN Assistant Secretary General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Regional Director for Africa
Ms. Ahunna Eziakonwa officially began her employment as UNDP's Assistant Administrator and Regional Director for Africa on August 15th, 2018. In this capacity, she manages UNDP's work to assist 46 African countries in achieving Agenda 2030 and the Sustainable Development Goals.

Ms. Eziakonwa-Onochie formerly served as UN Resident Coordinator, UNDP Resident Representative, and UN Humanitarian Coordinator in Ethiopia (2015-2018), Uganda (2012-2015), and Lesotho (2008-2012).

As Chief of the Africa Section of the Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (UNOCHA), she also managed more than 15 national operations in Africa. Before joining the UN, Ms. Eziakonwa had several high-ranking posts with African civil society organizations.

With a focus on the political and economic growth of Africa, Ms. Ahunna Eziakonwa-Onochie received her master's degree in international affairs from Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs in New York. Ms. Eziakonwa-Onochie is particularly passionate about increasing the chances for young people and women to take part in sustainable development.
Michelle Muschett
UN Assistant Secretary General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Regional Director for Latin America and the Caribbean
Michelle Muschett (Panama) is the United Nations Assistant Secretary-General, Assistant Administrator, and Director of the United Nations Development Programme's Regional Bureau for Latin America and the Caribbean.

She is a social policy and international development specialist with cross-sectoral experience in a variety of leadership positions.

During her tenure as Panama's deputy minister and minister of social development, Ms. Muschett supervised the construction of the first Latin American childhood MPI, the first national Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI), and the national endorsement of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) through public involvement and strategic collaborations.

She also held the position of chair of the National Concertation Council for Development, a debate forum whose goal was to encourage consensus among various societal groups on national development priorities.

In her previous role, Ms. Muschett served as Senior Public Policy Advisor and Executive Education Director for Oxford's Poverty and Human Development Initiative (OPHI), where she advised governments and policymakers in Latin America, Africa, and Asia on ways to address multidimensional poverty. Additionally, she works as the Senior Policy and Strategy Advisor for the Fourth Sector Group, providing guidance on how to enlist group leadership and action to promote business models that could benefit society and the environment as well as how to use development finance to speed the transition to more inclusive, resilient, and sustainable economic systems around the world.

She has lectured and taught in universities in Asia, Europe, and Latin America. Political innovation, human and social development, and global civic leadership are among her areas of competence. She is a member of the Aspen Global Leadership Network, the Central America Leadership Initiative, and the International Women's Forum's Panama Chapter.

Ms. Muschett's work is focused on international arbitration and corporate law. She has also contributed to the development of federal legislation.

Ms. Muschett holds master's degrees in public administration from Cornell University in the United States, in commercial law from Externado University in Colombia, and in management of cultural heritage projects from the Institute for Art and Restoration Palazzo Spinelli in Italy. She speaks Italian, Spanish, and English fluently
Ulrika Modéer
UN Assistant Secretary General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director of the Bureau of External Relations and Advocacy
Ms. Ulrika Modéer assumed the roles of UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director of the Bureau of External Relations and Advocacy on August 20, 2018. In her position, she guides the organization in developing vital ties with Member States as well as fresh and up-and-coming partners. She oversees UNDP's communications and advocacy initiatives to advance the vision of the Sustainable Development Goals.

Previously, Ms. Modéer held the position of State Secretary for International Development Cooperation and Climate for Sweden, where she played a critical role in reorganizing the nation's international development cooperation to advance the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

She has experience working in both Latin America (Bolivia, Guatemala) and Africa (Mozambique, South Africa), has a strong background in policy, and is knowledgeable in parliamentary processes and civil society.

Ms. Modéer received her BA in International Relations from the University of Gothenburg in Sweden. The mother of three children, Ms. Modéer was born in 1969.
Asako Okai
UN Assistant Secretary General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director of the Crisis Bureau
On August 22, Ms. Asako Okai took over as UNDP's Assistant Administrator and Director of the Crisis Bureau. She manages UNDP's commercial crisis work and develops the organization's strategy and goals for crisis prevention, response, and recovery.

Ms. Okai has over 30 years of experience with the United Nations and the Japanese Foreign Service. She has extensive expertise working at both the strategic and operational levels in development, humanitarian aid, crisis management, and peacebuilding.

Kanni Wignaraja
UN Assistant Secretary General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director of the UNDP Regional Bureau for Asia and the Pacific
Ms. Kanni Wignaraja commenced as the UNDP's Assistant Administrator and Director of the Regional Bureau for Asia and the Pacific on November 18, 2019. Ms. Wignaraja previously held the roles of Acting Assistant Administrator, Director of the UNDP's Bureau of Management Services, and Special Adviser to the UNDP Administrator, having served as Director of the UN Development Operations Coordination Office from 2014 to 2018. She continued to carry out these responsibilities into 2019.

Ms. Wignaraja has over 25 years of experience with UNDP's engagement in the SDGs and the UN mission. She is well-versed in the Asia-Pacific area, as well as UNDP operations, management, and policy at the global, regional, and country levels.

Before joining the UN, Kanni was employed by the Ford Foundation in New York. Kanni has contributed to a variety of papers, publications, and conferences in the areas of leadership, institutional reform, capacity building, human rights, and public policy.

Ms. Wignaraja holds degrees in public administration (development economics) from Princeton University and a master's in arts in economics from Bryn Mawr College. She is married and has two children.
Haoliang Xu
UN Assistant Secretary-General, UNDP Assistant Administrator and Director of Bureau for Policy and Programme Support
Haoliang Xu was appointed by the UN Secretary-General as Assistant Secretary-General, Assistant Administrator, and Director for the Bureau for Policy and Programme Support in July 2019.

Mr. Xu formerly held the positions of Assistant Secretary-General for the UN, Assistant Administrator for the UNDP, and Director for the Asia and Pacific area.

Prior to this, he served as Deputy Regional Director of the Regional Bureau for Europe and the Commonwealth of Independent States in New York since 2010; UN Resident Coordinator and UNDP Resident Representative in Kazakhstan (2007-2010); Country Director in Pakistan (2005-2007); Deputy Resident Representative for Programme, Pakistan (2004-2005); Country Manager and Senior Deputy Resident Representative, UNDP Timor-Leste (2002-2004); Deputy Resident Representative, UNDP Iran (2000-2002); Programme Manager, Regional Bureau for Asia and the Pacific, New York (1997-1999); Programme Officer/Junior Professional Officer, UNDP Kazakhstan (1995-1997).

Prior to working for the UN, Mr. Xu was employed as a computer-aided design engineer by Louis Berger International Inc. in New Jersey and as an assistant instructor at Tongji University in Shanghai, China.

Chinese national Mr. Xu holds engineering bachelor's and master's degrees from Tongji University in Shanghai and Stevens Institute of Technology in the United States, respectively. He also holds a Master of International Affairs in Economic Development and Policy Analysis from Columbia University (USA).
